# CPE 301 - Professional Issues in Computer Engineering

Professional and ethical issues related to the work in computer engineering. Personal attributes including personal development, interpersonal communication skills, employer expectations, writing and presentation skills. Social and ethical issues including business ethics and practices. Laws issues including computer-act laws, sensor and privacy, intelligence property, copyrights and patents, information protection, health and safety. Business issues including business organization, finance and account, resource management.

## Course Competencies

- Identify and explain different types of business and organizational structures, including sole proprietorships, partnerships, limited/public companies, tall, flat, division-based, and cross-functional structures, along with their respective advantages and disadvantages.
- Describe potential career paths for tech professionals and differentiate between the roles of a coach, mentor, and sponsor in professional development.
- List and explain ten key attributes employers expect from their employees, including a positive attitude, punctuality, dependability, desire to grow, effective communication, meeting deadlines, self-motivation, and learning from mistakes.
- Examine and differentiate between employment contracts, Non-Disclosure Agreements (NDAs), and confidentiality and non-competition clauses.
- Detail the core functions and responsibilities of various corporate departments, such as Human Resources, Operations, Information Technology, Marketing, Sales, Accounting and Finance, Research and Development, Customer Services, and Legal.
- Discuss core ethical principles in business, including honesty, trust, fairness, and responsibility, and identify unethical business behaviors to avoid, such as bribery, plagiarism, child labor, accounting fraud, and false advertising.
- Explain the frameworks of Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) and Environmental, Social, and Corporate Governance (ESG) criteria.
- Analyze the environmental and social impacts of technology, including issues related to pollution and e-waste, social media, artificial intelligence, and electric vehicles.
- Provide an overview of digital laws in Thailand, focusing on the Computer-Related Crime Act, the Cybersecurity Act, and the Personal Data Protection Act (PDPA).
- Explain the fundamentals of income tax, including who is required to pay and what constitutes taxable income from both domestic and international sources.
