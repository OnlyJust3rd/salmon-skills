# CPE 486 - Game Design and Development

This class focuses on the process of planning, developing, and creating computer games. Students will gain an understanding of the computer game industry, and will learn how to design and develop game characters, levels, and game design documents.

## Course Competencies

- Describe the standard phases of the computer game design and development life cycle, from initial concept to post-launch maintenance.
- Categorize computer games into distinct genres based on their core gameplay mechanics and intended audiences.
- Identify and explain the fundamental building blocks of a game, including its rules, objectives, core mechanics, and interactive systems.
- Explain the principles of natural funativity theory and its connection to player enjoyment.
- Apply Maslow's hierarchy of needs as a framework to understand player motivation and reward systems in game design.
- Analyze the role of storytelling in building emotional engagement within computer games.
- Design game characters, encompassing their visual appearance, psychological traits, and gameplay mechanics.
- Create scene designs, considering visual composition, lighting, and artistic layout to establish mood and tone.
- Develop level designs, focusing on spatial layout, pacing, and the placement of interactive elements.
- Formulate game design principles that integrate rules, content, and mechanics to deliver an intended player experience.
- Balance game flow and difficulty to maintain player immersion and prevent frustration.
