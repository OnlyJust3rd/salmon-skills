# CPE 111 - Discrete Mathematics for Computer Engineers

This course provides a comprehensive study of discrete mathematics for computer engineers, covering all major topics in the syllabus. It begins with logic and Boolean algebra, including propositional and predicate logic, logical reasoning, and algebraic structures used in digital systems. Students study fundamental mathematical structures such as sets, functions, sequences, sums, and matrices, followed by algorithm design and number theory, including integer representations. The course emphasizes formal reasoning techniques including mathematical induction and recursion. Combinatorial topics include counting principles, permutations, combinations, discrete probability, and advanced counting techniques. Students explore relations and their properties as a basis for data representation. The course further examines graph theory and tree structures, including their properties and related algorithms. Finally, it introduces theoretical models of computation, including finite automata, math prefix/postfix-notation, establishing the foundation for understanding computation.

## Course Competencies

- Apply propositional and predicate logic to represent and reason about problems.
- Use sets, functions, sequences, sums, matrices, and relations to model discrete structures.
- Apply algorithmic thinking and number theory to solve computational problems.
- Use mathematical induction and recursion to prove correctness and analyze recursive processes.
- Solve combinatorial problems using counting principles, permutations, combinations, and discrete probability.
- Model and solve problems using graphs, trees, and related algorithms.
- Understand and describe computational models including finite automata.
