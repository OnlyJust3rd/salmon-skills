# MTH 101 - Mathematics I

Review function and their properties, number e, logarithm function, inverse function. Limit of function, computation of limits, continuous function . Basic concepts of derivative, derivative of algebraic function, the chain rule, derivatives of transcendental functions, derivatives of inverse function, implicit differentiation, higher order derivatives, indeterminate form and L'Hopital's rule. Differentials, linear approximation, the max-min value theorem. Rolle's theorem and mean value theorem. Concavity and second derivative, using derivative and limits in sketching graph, applied max-min problem, related rates. Basic concepts of integrals, fundamental theorem of calculus, properties of antiderivatives and definite integrals, indefinite integral, integration by substitution, integration by parts, integration by partial fractions. Area under curve and areas between curves. Improper integrals, numerical Integration. Function of several variables, graph of equations. Partial derivative, differentials, the chain rule. Critical points, second order partial derivative, relative extrema, maxima and minima, and saddle points.

## Course Competencies

- Solve problems and express mathematical ideas coherently in written form based on mathematical logic, structuring clear, step-by-step mathematical proofs and problem-solving processes, and applying mathematical logic to justify the use of specific calculus rules and theorems.
- Explain concepts in functions of one or more variables and calculate inverse functions, limits, derivatives, maxima and minima, and linear approximation, reviewing and identifying properties of single-variable functions, including the number e, logarithmic functions, and inverse functions; computing limits of a function, limits involving infinity, and determining the continuity of functions; calculating derivatives of algebraic and transcendental functions using standard rules like the chain rule; performing implicit differentiation and computing higher-order derivatives; and calculating differentials and estimating values using linear approximation.
- Explain concepts and how to use the theorems that apply specifically to continuous functions (intermediate value theorem, extreme value theorem) and to differentiable functions (chain rule, Rolle's theorem, mean value theorem, l'Hopital's rule), applying the Max-Min Value Theorem, Rolle's theorem, and the Mean Value Theorem to analyze the behavior of differentiable functions, and evaluating limits of indeterminate forms using L'Hopital's rule.
- Explain the concepts of differential calculus of functions of two or more variables, continuity, partial differentiation, chain rule, and implicit differentiation, analyzing functions of several variables, sketching graphs of equations, and evaluating their limits and continuity; computing first and second-order partial derivatives and differentials; applying the chain rule to multivariable functions; and identifying critical points and evaluating relative extrema, maxima, minima, and saddle points for multivariable functions.
- Find anti-derivatives by using standard techniques, evaluating indefinite and definite integrals; applying various integration techniques, including integration by substitution, integration by parts, and integration of rational functions using partial fractions; and evaluating improper integrals and applying numerical integration techniques.
- Describe how the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus can be used both to evaluate integrals and to define new functions, and determine their basic properties, explaining the basic concepts of integrals and properties of antiderivatives, and applying the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus and average values to transition between differentiation and integration.
- Apply calculus concepts in related rates, minimum and maximum problems, graph sketching, area, and volume, formulating and solving real-world applied maximum-minimum problems and related rates problems; using first and second derivatives to determine increasing/decreasing intervals, concavity, and inflection points to accurately sketch graphs of curves; and applying definite integrals to calculate the area under a curve and the area between curves.
