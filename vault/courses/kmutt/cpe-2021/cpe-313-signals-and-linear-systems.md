# CPE 313 - Signals and Linear Systems

The "Signals and Linear Systems" course provides an introduction to signals and systems, focusing on their mathematical representation in the time domain, linear time-invariant (LTI) systems, and linear feedback systems. Students will learn to convert continuous-time signals to discrete-time signals and will analyze signals in frequency and complex frequency domains using Fourier series, continuous-time and discrete-time Fourier transforms, Laplace transforms, and Z-transforms.

## Course Competencies

- Define and represent continuous-time and discrete-time signals using mathematical models and functions.
- Classify signals into types such as continuous vs. discrete, periodic vs. non-periodic, and exponential vs. sinusoidal, and identify unit impulse and unit step functions.
- Evaluate basic properties of systems, including static/dynamic, causal, stable (bounded input, bounded output), invertible, linear, and time-invariant characteristics.
- Determine the impulse response of Linear Time-Invariant (LTI) systems.
- Perform Discrete-Time (DT) convolution by applying the mathematical process of changing independent variables, flipping, shifting, multiplying, and summing sequences.
- Calculate Continuous-Time (CT) convolution by integrating the product of two continuous signals over time.
- Decompose periodic signals into sine and cosine terms using the Fourier Series.
- Find Fourier Series (FS) and Discrete-Time Fourier Series (DTFS) coefficients and apply mathematical properties such as linearity, time-shifting, and Parseval's Relation.
- Apply the Fourier Transform (FT) and Discrete-Time Fourier Transform (DTFT) to analyze aperiodic signals in the frequency domain.
- Calculate the magnitude and phase representation of the FT and DTFT for frequency response analysis.
- Define the Region of Convergence (ROC) for Laplace Transforms and calculate poles and zeros.
- Geometrically evaluate the Fourier Transform from a pole-zero plot to determine causality and stability of continuous-time systems.
- Find the Region of Convergence (ROC) for Z-Transforms, plot poles and zeros, and calculate inverse Z-transforms using long division.
- Assess system stability using Z-transforms.
