# MTH 102 - Mathematics II

Scalars and Vectors, Inner Product, Vectors Product, Scalar Triple Product, Line and Plane in 3-Space. Mathematical Induction, Sequences, Series, The Integral Test, The Comparison Test, The Ratio Test, The Alternating Series and Absolute Convergence Tests, Binomial Expansion, Power Series, Taylor's Formula. Periodic Functions, Fourier Series, Polar Coordinates, Areas in Polar Coordinates, Definite Integral over Plane and Solid Regions, Double Integrals, Double Integrals in Polar Form, Transformation of Variable in Multiple Integrals, Triple Integrals in Rectangular Coordinates, Triple Integrals in Cylindrical and Spherical Coordinates.

## Course Competencies

- Differentiate between scalar and vector quantities and execute fundamental vector operations, including calculating the inner (dot) product, vector (cross) product, and scalar triple product.
- Determine the magnitude (norm) of vectors, find unit vectors, and calculate the angle and orthogonal projections between vectors.
- Formulate the equations of lines and planes in 3-dimensional space using parametric equations and normal vectors.
- Analyze the geometric relationships in 3D space, such as identifying if lines or planes are parallel or orthogonal, finding intersections, and calculating the shortest distance between points, lines, and planes.
- Construct rigorous mathematical proofs for given statements or formulas using the principle of mathematical induction.
- Define and evaluate different types of sequences, particularly focusing on determining if a sequence is monotonically increasing/decreasing, bounded, and whether it converges to a specific limit or diverges.
- Apply appropriate convergence tests-such as the Integral Test, Comparison Test, Limit Comparison Test, Ratio Test, and Root Test-to evaluate infinite series.
- Distinguish between absolute and conditional convergence for alternating series and apply the Alternating Series Test appropriately.
- Analyze Power Series by calculating their specific radius and interval of convergence.
- Expand standard functions into Taylor and Maclaurin series, and utilize these polynomial approximations for functional evaluation.
- Apply Binomial expansion to functions and evaluate their interval of convergence.
- Define the properties of periodic functions and compute their Fourier series representations, including calculating the specific Fourier coefficients (a0, an, bn).
- Convert coordinates and equations between rectangular and polar systems.
- Sketch complex curves in the polar coordinate system (such as Cardioids, Limaçons, Rose Curves, Lemniscates, and Spirals) by utilizing symmetric properties to simplify the graphing process.
- Formulate and calculate definite integrals to find the area of regions bounded by one or multiple polar curves.
- Set up and evaluate double integrals over plane regions and triple integrals over solid regions using rectangular coordinates.
- Execute transformations of variables in multiple integrals by converting double integrals into polar form, and triple integrals into cylindrical or spherical coordinates to solve complex area and volume problems efficiently.
