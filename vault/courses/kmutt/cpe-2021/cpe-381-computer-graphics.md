# CPE 381 - Computer Graphics

This course introduces the basic concepts in computer graphics, covering the techniques for the creation, storage, transformation, and animation of computer models and images. Students will be introduced to line and polygon drawing algorithms, windowing and clipping, and fundamental 2D and 3D geometric transformations such as translation, scaling, and rotation. The curriculum places a strong emphasis on the 3D aspects of computer graphics, detailing parallel and perspective projections, what makes an image appear three-dimensional, and various techniques used for visual realism.

## Course Competencies

- Explain the basic concepts in computer graphics.
- Describe techniques for the creation, storage, transformation, and animation of computer models and images.
- Apply line and polygon drawing algorithms.
- Implement fundamental 2D geometric transformations such as translation, scaling, and rotation.
- Implement fundamental 3D geometric transformations such as translation, scaling, and rotation.
- Utilize windowing and clipping techniques.
- Differentiate between parallel and perspective projections.
- Analyze techniques that contribute to the appearance of three-dimensionality in an image.
- Evaluate various techniques used for achieving visual realism in 3-D computer graphics.
