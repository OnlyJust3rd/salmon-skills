# N/A - Cryptographic Techniques for Multimedia Communication

This course introduces concepts and goals of various cryptographic techniques for multimedia communication including texts, speech, images and video. Students will learn how to design and implement those techniques for a practical system. Topics covered include cryptographic algorithms and techniques, applied cryptography, speech security, principles of image and video compression, selective encryption, copyright violation problem, digital watermarking concepts & examples.

## Course Competencies

- Explain the core principles of cryptography and communication security, specifically secrecy, authentication, and integrity.
- Differentiate between symmetric key algorithms (classical/modern block ciphers and stream ciphers) and asymmetric key algorithms.
- Apply fundamental number theory concepts-such as modular arithmetic, greatest common divisor (gcd), Euclidean algorithms, and Euler's Phi function-to solve cryptographic problems.
- Analyze the step-by-step operations of modern block ciphers like DES, IDEA, and AES (including SubBytes, ShiftRows, MixColumns, and AddRoundKey processes).
- Describe the mechanics of stream ciphers, specifically Linear Feedback Shift Registers (LFSR), combining bit-stream generators (like Geffe), and the unbreakable One-time Pad.
- Illustrate the mechanics and mathematical foundations of public key algorithms, including Diffie-Hellman Key Agreement, El Gamal, and RSA.
- Describe the properties of cryptographic one-way hash functions (e.g., MD5 and SHA-1) and explain how they map variable-length data to fixed-length digests.
- Explain the construction and purpose of Message Authentication Codes (MAC) and Hash-based MAC (HMAC) for ensuring data integrity and user authentication.
- Detail the signing and verifying processes of Digital Signatures using algorithms like RSA and El Gamal.
- Evaluate applied cryptography in network protocols such as IPSec (AH and ESP in transport/tunnel modes), SSL/TLS, PGP, and Kerberos across different OSI layers.
- Analyze access control techniques and network defense mechanisms, differentiating between packet-filter firewalls and proxy firewalls.
- Assess the advantages and vulnerabilities of speech security systems, comparing digital ciphering against analogue scrambling techniques like speech inversion, band scramblers, and time element scramblers.
- Compare lossless compression algorithms (Huffman coding, Adaptive Huffman, LZW) with lossy compression methods (DPCM, Transform coding).
- Describe the major steps of the JPEG image compression standard (DCT, Quantization, Zig-zag scan, Entropy coding) and compare its operation modes (Sequential, Lossless, Progressive, Hierarchical).
- Explain video compression concepts, focusing on spatial and temporal redundancy removal via Intra-frames (I) and Inter-frames (P, B) using motion vectors in the MPEG and H.261/H.263 standards.
- Summarize audio compression techniques, including psychoacoustics (frequency and temporal masking) and the subband filtering process used in MPEG Audio.
- Define the goals of selective encryption (reducing computation while maintaining sufficient security) for resource-constrained multimedia communications like mobile devices and real-time networking.
- Classify and criticize selective encryption algorithms based on when encryption is performed relative to the compression pipeline: precompression, in-compression, and postcompression.
- Analyze the evaluation criteria for selective encryption systems, including tunability, visual degradation, cryptographic security, encryption ratio, format compliance, and error tolerance.
- Distinguish between access control, use control, and digital watermarking as technical solutions for copyright protection.
- Classify information hiding techniques (steganography vs. digital watermarking) and categorize watermarking types (e.g., visible/invisible, robust/fragile, spatial/frequency domains).
- Evaluate the inherent trade-offs in watermarking (imperceptibility vs. robustness vs. capacity) and measure watermarking performance using subjective (MOS) and objective metrics (MSE, PSNR, SSIM).
- Analyze potential attacks on digital watermarking systems-including removal, geometric, cryptography, and protocol attacks (e.g., copy attacks)-and explain the use of benchmark tools like StirMark.
