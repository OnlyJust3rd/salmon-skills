# CPE101 - Computer Engineering Exploration

Introduction to the field of Computer Engineering through hands-on, project-based learning. The course explores key domains such as software engineering, embedded systems, robotics, artificial intelligence, cybersecurity, operating systems, and computer networking, providing a broad view of real-world problem spaces.
The course emphasizes the engineering problem-solving process, including problem identification, requirement analysis, system design, prototyping, testing, and iterative improvement. Students apply fundamental concepts from mathematics and physics to design and implement practical solutions.
A central component of the course is robotics and embedded systems development, where students build systems using platforms such as microcontrollers, Micro:bit, and Arduino. Students gain experience in hardware-software integration, basic programming, and system-level thinking.
The course also introduces 2D/3D design and digital fabrication, including creating design models and producing physical components using 3D printing. These skills are integrated into project work to support rapid prototyping and iterative design.
Students work in teams to plan and execute projects, focusing on team collaboration, project planning, and technical communication. The course includes hands-on labs, design exercises, and team-based projects that simulate real-world engineering workflows.

## Course Competencies

- Describe and differentiate major domains in Computer Engineering (e.g., software engineering, embedded systems, robotics, AI, cybersecurity, operating systems, networking) and their typical problem contexts.
- Apply the engineering problem-solving process to Computer Engineering problems, including problem definition and requirement specification, system-level design (hardware and/or software), prototype development, and testing and iterative improvement.
- Develop basic embedded and robotic systems using platforms such as microcontrollers, Micro:bit, or Arduino, including interfacing with sensors and actuators, writing simple control programs, and integrating hardware and software components.
- Create and utilize 2D/3D designs for engineering applications, including designing basic components using CAD tools, preparing models for fabrication, and producing physical parts using 3D printing.
- Apply fundamental mathematics and physics concepts to support system design, analysis, and implementation.
- Collaborate effectively in teams to execute engineering projects, including task decomposition, role assignment, timeline planning, and coordination.
- Demonstrate basic project management skills, including planning, progress tracking, and iterative development.
- Communicate technical ideas and project results through structured reports, presentations, and visual artifacts.
- Design, implement, and present a functional team project that integrates embedded/robotic system development, 2D/3D design and physical prototyping, and the engineering problem-solving process.
