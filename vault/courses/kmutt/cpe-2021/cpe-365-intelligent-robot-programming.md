# CPE 365 - Intelligent Robot Programming

The CPE 365 Intelligent Robot Programming course is designed to familiarize students with the fundamental components of intelligent systems for mobile robot programming. By the end of the course, students will be able to design and implement their own intelligent systems using a simple mobile robot. The curriculum provides both hands-on experience and theoretical knowledge, introducing frameworks that connect various approaches within the research field of Artificial Intelligence (AI). Students are encouraged to explore a broad range of AI applications, with a special focus on the soft-computing area, to take their first steps toward AI research using robot programming as the primary task.

## Course Competencies

- Introduce students to the fundamental concepts of robotics through discussion of "The Robot at First Sight".
- Explain the fusion of soft computing and hard computing in industrial applications.
- Describe reactive control paradigms by exploring "Reactive Robotic Systems".
- Define fuzzy logic and fuzzy controllers based on a "Fuzzy Logic Introduction".
- Apply fuzzy logic control for robot maze traversal using the SIMBot.
- Discuss alternative AI behavior-based paradigms by analyzing Rodney A. Brooks' "Elephants Don't Play Chess".
- Explain foundational concepts of Neural Networks from the first three chapters of the text by Ben Krose and Patrick van der Smagt.
- Explore further into Artificial Neural Networks by reading chapter four of the text by Ben Krose and Patrick van der Smagt.
- Analyze how intelligent robotic behavior can be achieved without traditional, complex internal world models by discussing Rodney A. Brooks' "Intelligence Without Representation".
- Learn about software algorithms and hardware implementations required for mobile robots to navigate their environments for path planning.
- Explore the biological inspirations for computational evolution by discussing "Charles Darwin" and the "Evolution of Evolution Theory".
- Apply genetic programming to robotics, specifically examining the "Evolution of Subsumption Using Genetic Programming".
- Discuss how to transition models from simulation to the real world using anticipation in "Evolutionary Robotics".
- Address the practical challenges of deploying simulated intelligence programs onto physical, real-world robots.
- Evaluate all course materials discussed over the semester during "Final Conclusions".
