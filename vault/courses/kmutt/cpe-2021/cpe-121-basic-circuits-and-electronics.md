# CPE 121 - Basic Circuits and Electronics

Electrical units and definitions, fundamental laws, resistive circuits and networks, reactive circuits and networks, frequency response, sinusoidal analysis. Introduction to semiconductor devices; diodes, diode circuits, bipolar junction transistor, field-effect transistor, DC biasing and AC small-signal analysis of transistor amplifiers, operational amplifiers, filters. Safety considerations on electronic measurement equipment. Experiments on some useful electrical and electronic circuits.

## Course Competencies

- Formulate and solve for circuit parameters using fundamental theorems such as Mesh/Nodal analysis, Superposition, Thévenin, and Norton.
- Analyze the transient behavior of energy storage elements, computing time constants and step responses for RC and RL circuits.
- Transform sinusoidal signals into phasors to analyze AC circuits in the steady-state domain.
- Safely operate standard measurement tools, including digital multimeters, oscilloscopes, and function generators, to read resistor color codes, measure voltages/currents, and capture signals.
- Design multi-stage Operational Amplifier circuits to achieve specific amplification gains and signal inversion requirements.
- Design and test passive RC and RL frequency filters to remove noise from signals based on specific cutoff frequencies.
- Design, construct, and test diode-based circuits, including logic gates and rectifiers, evaluating their true output against expected logic states.
- Record experimental values, calculate expected theoretical values, and accurately compute the percentage error to evaluate measurement precision.
- Discuss and justify the causes of signal distortion or discrepancies between theoretical calculations and actual physical measurements within formal lab reports.
