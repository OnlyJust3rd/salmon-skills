# CPE 363 - Human-Computer Interaction

This course introduces students to Human-Computer Interaction (HCI), an interdisciplinary field focused on understanding, designing, and evaluating how people interact with digital systems. Students will learn principles of interaction design, usability evaluation, and user experience (UX) research, exploring theoretical foundations and applying skills through hands-on projects.

## Course Competencies

- Distinguish between good and bad user interface designs based on historical trends and the experience economy.
- Explain the cognitive foundations of human-computer interaction, including sensory processing, memory, and attention.
- Apply the four basic activities of Interaction Design: discovering requirements, designing alternatives, prototyping, and evaluating.
- Utilize techniques such as personas, scenarios, storyboarding, and use cases to bring requirements to life.
- Apply design principles for maximum usability, including learnability, flexibility, and robustness, and review established guidelines and heuristics.
- Differentiate between conceptual design and concrete design in prototyping, outlining typical development steps.
- Build prototypes using physical computing toolkits like Arduino and develop Extended Reality (XR) applications.
- Explain the characteristics of Virtual Reality (VR) and Augmented Reality (AR) within the context of the virtuality continuum.
- Explore visual concepts in HCI, including human perception, visual hierarchy, visual aesthetics, data visualization, and UI design encoding.
- Design and conduct HCI experiments, formulating hypotheses, defining tasks, and identifying variables (independent, dependent, control, and confounding).
- Mitigate order and learning effects in experiments using methods like Latin squares.
- Analyze data and interpret the results of usability evaluations using statistical foundations.
- Discuss advanced themes in HCI, including social interactions, conversational technologies, workspace awareness, and social presence.
- Identify future capabilities, trends, and ethical considerations within human-computer interaction.
- Design, develop, and evaluate computer-based devices through a team project.
