# CPE 494 - Special Topic IV: Commercializing Your Projects

This course is designed to help students transform their projects into viable businesses by applying lean startup methodologies to validate business ideas and developing robust business models and strategies. Students will build a strong entrepreneurial mindset, focusing on opportunity recognition, problem-solving, and creative and analytical thinking. Grounded in the principle that innovation equals creating something new that solves a real problem, the course operates on the philosophy that "learning is best when put into practice". Beyond just startup fundamentals, students will also cultivate essential interpersonal skills, including resilience, adaptability, empathy, teamwork, and communication.

## Course Competencies

- Apply lean startup methodologies to validate business ideas.
- Develop robust business models and strategies for project commercialization.
- Demonstrate an entrepreneurial mindset by identifying opportunities and solving problems.
- Utilize creative and analytical thinking to foster innovation.
- Explain the core framework of idea validation and the Build, Measure, Learn principle.
- Analyze early adopters and map the Customer Journey to understand target customers.
- Conduct Problem Interviews to validate assumptions and identify customer pain points.
- Evaluate market size and perform competitive analysis.
- Map business components using the Lean Canvas framework.
- Build Proof of Concepts and a Minimum Viable Product (MVP) with essential features.
- Develop User Experience (UX) for a product or service.
- Conduct Solution Interviews and Usability Testing to validate an MVP.
- Structure a business using the Business Model Canvas, including defining an Unfair Advantage.
- Explain the Technology Adoption Lifecycle and the Marketing Mix (4Ps vs. 4Cs).
- Assess project feasibility through profitability analysis, financial returns, and risk assessment.
- Identify foundational legal, IP, and regulatory considerations for businesses.
- Examine organizational behavior principles including Culture Building, Team Dynamics, Communication, Leadership, and Change Management.
- Apply pitching techniques to effectively communicate a startup idea and its value proposition.
- Demonstrate resilience, adaptability, empathy, teamwork, and communication skills.
