# Capturing Collaboration Feedback: Figma's Design Comment Feature

As you move towards creating interactive prototypes in Figma, gathering and acting on feedback is crucial. The **Design Comment** feature in Figma is your primary tool for capturing this collaboration. It allows stakeholders, teammates, and even your future self to leave contextual feedback directly on your designs, making it easier to understand issues and iterate effectively.

## Why Use Design Comments?

When building interactive prototypes, the user experience is paramount. Feedback helps identify usability issues, inconsistencies, and areas for improvement that might not be obvious during the design process. Design comments offer:

*   **Context:** Feedback is tied to a specific element or area on the canvas, eliminating guesswork.
*   **Traceability:** You can see who said what and when, creating a history of design decisions.
*   **Actionability:** Comments can be marked as resolved, helping you track progress.
*   **Collaboration:** It's a central place for everyone involved to contribute their thoughts.

## How to Add and Manage Comments

Figma's commenting system is straightforward and integrated directly into the design workflow.

### Adding a Comment

1.  **Select the Comment Tool:** In the top toolbar, click on the speech bubble icon (it looks like a comment bubble).
2.  **Place the Comment Pin:** Click on the specific area of your design where you want to leave feedback. A pin will appear.
3.  **Type Your Comment:** A text field will appear next to the pin. Write your feedback clearly and concisely.
    *   **Be Specific:** Instead of "This button looks bad," try "This button's contrast with the background makes it difficult to read."
    *   **Suggest Solutions (Optional):** If you have an idea for improvement, include it. "Consider increasing the font size of the heading by 2px for better readability."
    *   **@Mention Teammates:** To notify specific individuals, type `@` followed by their name. This will send them an email notification.
4.  **Post the Comment:** Click the "Post" button or press `Enter`.

### Viewing Comments

*   **Comment Mode:** When you are in comment mode (the speech bubble icon is active), all existing comments will be visible as pins on the canvas.
*   **Clicking a Pin:** Clicking on a comment pin will open the full comment thread.
*   **Comment Panel:** On the right-hand sidebar, you'll see a "Comments" tab. Clicking this will display a list of all comments on the current page, allowing you to quickly scan or jump to specific feedback.

### Resolving Comments

Once you've addressed a comment (e.g., made the suggested design change), it's good practice to mark it as resolved.

1.  **Locate the Comment:** Click on the comment pin or find it in the Comments panel.
2.  **Click "Mark as Resolved":** Within the comment thread, you'll find a "Mark as resolved" button or checkbox. Clicking this will visually de-emphasize the comment, indicating it's no longer an active issue. Resolved comments can still be viewed by toggling the "Show resolved comments" option in the Comments panel.

## Best Practices for Effective Commenting

To maximize the value of the Design Comment feature:

*   **Be Constructive:** Frame feedback as suggestions for improvement rather than criticisms.
*   **Prioritize:** If there are many comments, focus on the most critical ones first.
*   **Respond Promptly:** Acknowledge comments, ask clarifying questions, or indicate when you'll address them.
*   **Use @Mentions Wisely:** Only mention individuals who need to be directly involved in that specific feedback.
*   **Keep it Concise:** Long, rambling comments are less likely to be read and acted upon.
*   **Add Visuals (When Needed):** You can use Figma's annotation tools or even add a screenshot within a comment if a visual explanation is necessary.

By effectively using Figma's Design Comment feature, you transform static designs into a dynamic space for collaborative iteration, ultimately leading to stronger and more user-centered interactive prototypes.
