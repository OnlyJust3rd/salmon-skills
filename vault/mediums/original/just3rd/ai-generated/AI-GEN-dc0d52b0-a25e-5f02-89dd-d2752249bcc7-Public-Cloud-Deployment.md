# Public Cloud Deployment

This lesson focuses on understanding the public cloud deployment model, a core component of differentiating cloud models.

## What is Public Cloud?

The public cloud is a computing model where third-party providers make computing resources, such as servers and storage, available to the general public over the internet. Think of it like a utility company providing electricity or water. You pay for what you use, and the provider manages the underlying infrastructure.

Key characteristics of public cloud include:

*   **Accessibility:** Services are available over the public internet, making them easily accessible from anywhere with an internet connection.
*   **Scalability:** Resources can be scaled up or down rapidly based on demand, offering immense flexibility.
*   **Pay-as-you-go:** You are typically billed based on your consumption of resources, often by the hour, minute, or gigabyte.
*   **Shared Infrastructure:** Resources are shared among multiple tenants (users or organizations), though isolation mechanisms ensure data security.

## Practical Scenario: Launching a New E-commerce Website

Imagine you're a startup launching a new e-commerce website. You anticipate a surge in traffic during a holiday sale, followed by lower traffic at other times.

**Using Public Cloud:**

1.  **Resource Provisioning:** Instead of buying and setting up your own servers, you can rent virtual machines (servers), storage space, and databases from a public cloud provider like Amazon Web Services (AWS), Microsoft Azure, or Google Cloud Platform (GCP).
2.  **Scalability for Peak Load:** As the holiday sale approaches, you can easily increase the number of virtual servers to handle the expected traffic. After the sale, you can scale back down to save costs.
3.  **Managed Services:** Many public cloud providers offer managed services for databases, load balancers, and content delivery networks (CDNs). This means the provider handles the patching, backups, and maintenance of these services, freeing up your team to focus on building the website's features.
4.  **Cost-Effectiveness:** You only pay for the computing power, storage, and bandwidth you use during the sale. This is far more cost-effective than investing in hardware that would sit idle for most of the year.

In this scenario, the public cloud provides the agility and cost efficiency needed for a dynamic business like e-commerce.
