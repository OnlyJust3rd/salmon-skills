# Theorems for Continuous Functions

This lesson focuses on remembering the names of key theorems that apply specifically to continuous functions. Understanding these theorems is a foundational step in applying calculus.

## What are Continuous Functions?

Before diving into the theorems, it's helpful to recall what a continuous function is. Informally, a function is continuous if its graph can be drawn without lifting your pen from the paper. More formally, a function \(f(x)\) is continuous at a point \(c\) if:

1.  \(f(c)\) is defined.
2.  \(\lim_{x \to c} f(x)\) exists.
3.  \(\lim_{x \to c} f(x) = f(c)\).

Many familiar functions, like polynomials, sine, cosine, and exponential functions, are continuous everywhere.

## Key Theorems for Continuous Functions

For functions that are continuous over a given interval, certain powerful theorems hold true. Remembering their names is crucial for recognizing when and how they can be applied.

Here are some fundamental theorems that apply to continuous functions:

*   **Intermediate Value Theorem (IVT)**
*   **Extreme Value Theorem (EVT)**
*   **Mean Value Theorem (MVT)** (While not exclusively for continuous functions, it requires continuity on a closed interval)
*   **Rolle's Theorem** (A special case of the Mean Value Theorem)

### Practical Scenario

Imagine you are analyzing the temperature of a room throughout a day. You can reasonably assume that the temperature changes continuously; it doesn't suddenly jump from 20°C to 30°C instantaneously.

*   If you know the temperature was 20°C at 8 AM and 30°C at 2 PM, the **Intermediate Value Theorem** tells you that *every* temperature between 20°C and 30°C must have occurred at some point between 8 AM and 2 PM. You don't need to check every minute; the theorem guarantees it.
*   The **Extreme Value Theorem** guarantees that if the temperature is continuous over a closed interval (like the 12 hours from 8 AM to 8 PM), then there *must* be a highest temperature (maximum) and a lowest temperature (minimum) reached during that time.
