# Understanding Matrix Dimensions

In the world of Matrix Algebra, understanding the fundamental properties of matrices is crucial. One of the most basic yet important properties is a matrix's **dimension**. Knowing the dimensions tells us about the size of the matrix, much like knowing the length and width of a rectangle.

## What are Matrix Dimensions?

The dimension of a matrix is defined by its **number of rows** and its **number of columns**. We express the dimension of a matrix as "rows × columns".

*   A **row** is a horizontal line of numbers within the matrix.
*   A **column** is a vertical line of numbers within the matrix.

For example, a matrix with 3 rows and 2 columns has a dimension of 3 × 2.

## How to Identify Matrix Dimensions

To identify the dimensions of a matrix, you simply need to count:

1.  **Count the number of rows:** Look horizontally across the matrix and count how many distinct rows of numbers there are.
2.  **Count the number of columns:** Look vertically down the matrix and count how many distinct columns of numbers there are.

Remember to always state the dimension as "rows × columns". The order matters!

## Examples

Let's look at a few examples:

**Example 1:**

Consider the matrix $$A$$:
$$
A = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 5 \\ 1 & 8 \end{bmatrix}
$$

*   **Rows:** There are 2 horizontal lines of numbers.
*   **Columns:** There are 2 vertical lines of numbers.

Therefore, the dimension of matrix $$A$$ is **2 × 2**.

**Example 2:**

Consider the matrix $$B$$:
$$
B = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 0 & 7 \\ 4 & 1 & 6 \end{bmatrix}
$$

*   **Rows:** There are 2 horizontal lines of numbers.
*   **Columns:** There are 3 vertical lines of numbers.

Therefore, the dimension of matrix $$B$$ is **2 × 3**.

**Example 3:**

Consider the matrix $$C$$:
$$
C = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix}
$$

*   **Rows:** There are 3 horizontal lines of numbers.
*   **Columns:** There is 1 vertical line of numbers.

Therefore, the dimension of matrix $$C$$ is **3 × 1**. This type of matrix, with only one column, is also known as a column vector.

**Example 4:**

Consider the matrix $$D$$:
$$
D = \begin{bmatrix} 5 & 10 & 15 \end{bmatrix}
$$

*   **Rows:** There is 1 horizontal line of numbers.
*   **Columns:** There are 3 vertical lines of numbers.

Therefore, the dimension of matrix $$D$$ is **1 × 3**. This type of matrix, with only one row, is also known as a row vector.
