# Top-Down Market Sizing

This lesson introduces a common market sizing technique: **Top-Down Market Sizing**. This method helps estimate the potential size of a market by starting with broad industry data and narrowing it down.

## What is Top-Down Market Sizing?

Top-down market sizing begins with a large, well-defined market or industry and then progressively refines it to estimate the specific segment you are interested in. You use readily available data, such as reports from industry analysts or government statistics, and apply logical assumptions or calculations to arrive at your target market size.

This approach is useful when direct, granular data for your specific niche is scarce or difficult to obtain. It provides a quick initial estimate that can be refined later.

## How it Works: A Practical Example

Imagine you want to estimate the market size for premium, organic dog food in London.

1.  **Start with the broadest market:** The total pet food market in the UK.
    *   Let's say industry reports indicate the UK pet food market is worth £5 billion annually.

2.  **Narrow down by segment:** Focus on the dog food segment.
    *   Assume dog food represents 70% of the total pet food market.
    *   Calculation: £5 billion \* 0.70 = £3.5 billion (UK Dog Food Market).

3.  **Narrow down by geography:** Focus on London.
    *   London's population is roughly 15% of the UK's total population. While not a direct correlation, let's assume the dog food market share in London is proportional to its population.
    *   Calculation: £3.5 billion \* 0.15 = £525 million (London Dog Food Market).

4.  **Narrow down by product type/value:** Focus on premium, organic dog food.
    *   Industry research or surveys might suggest that premium and organic dog food accounts for 20% of the dog food market in affluent urban areas like London.
    *   Calculation: £525 million \* 0.20 = £105 million (Premium, Organic Dog Food Market in London).

This top-down approach has provided an estimated market size of £105 million for premium, organic dog food in London.

## When to Use Top-Down Market Sizing

*   When you need a quick, initial estimate of market size.
*   When granular data for your specific niche is unavailable.
*   When you have access to reliable broad market data (e.g., industry reports, government statistics).
*   As a starting point before diving into more detailed bottom-up analysis.
