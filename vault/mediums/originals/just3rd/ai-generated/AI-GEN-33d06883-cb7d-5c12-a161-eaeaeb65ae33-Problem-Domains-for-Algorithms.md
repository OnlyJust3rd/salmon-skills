# Problem Domains for Algorithms

Algorithms are powerful tools that help us solve problems. But not all problems are the same! Algorithms are designed to tackle different kinds of challenges. Understanding these problem domains helps us choose the right algorithm for the job.

## What Kinds of Problems Can Algorithms Solve?

Algorithms are built to find solutions in various areas. Here are some common problem domains:

*   **Searching:** Finding a specific item within a collection of data. Think of looking for a particular book in a library or a contact on your phone.
*   **Sorting:** Arranging data in a specific order, like alphabetically, numerically, or by date. This is useful for organizing lists, reports, or search results.
*   **Graph Problems:** Dealing with networks and relationships between entities. This includes finding the shortest path between two points (like in GPS navigation) or analyzing social networks.
*   **Optimization:** Finding the best possible solution among many options. This could be determining the most efficient delivery route for packages or allocating resources to maximize profit.
*   **String Processing:** Working with text data. This includes tasks like searching for patterns within text, translating languages, or checking for plagiarism.
*   **Computational Geometry:** Solving problems related to geometric shapes and their properties. Examples include finding the closest pair of points or determining if a point lies inside a polygon.

## A Practical Scenario

Imagine you're planning a road trip. You have a list of cities you want to visit.

*   **Searching:** You need to find information about each city (e.g., hotel availability, attractions). This is a searching problem.
*   **Sorting:** You want to arrange the cities in your itinerary based on their geographical proximity to minimize travel time. This is a sorting problem.
*   **Graph Problems:** The road network between cities can be represented as a graph. You want to find the shortest route to visit all cities. This is a classic graph problem.
*   **Optimization:** You might also want to optimize your trip to spend the least amount of money on fuel while visiting all your desired locations. This is an optimization problem.
