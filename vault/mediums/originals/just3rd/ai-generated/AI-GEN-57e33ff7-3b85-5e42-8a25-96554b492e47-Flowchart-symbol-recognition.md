# Flowchart Symbol Recognition

Understanding how to represent algorithms visually is a key part of algorithm representation. Flowcharts are a common way to do this, and they use standardized symbols to show different steps and decisions. This lesson focuses on recognizing and naming these standard flowchart symbols.

## What are Flowchart Symbols?

Flowchart symbols are graphical shapes that represent specific actions or operations within an algorithm. By using these symbols consistently, anyone reading a flowchart can understand the sequence and logic of the process.

## Common Flowchart Symbols

Here are some of the most common symbols you'll encounter:

| Symbol | Name | Description |
|---|---|---|
| ![Terminator Symbol](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a0/Flowchart_start_stop.svg/100px-Flowchart_start_stop.svg.png) | Terminator | Represents the start or end of a flowchart. |
| ![Process Symbol](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b7/Flowchart_process.svg/100px-Flowchart_process.svg.png) | Process | Represents an action or operation, like a calculation or assignment. |
| ![Decision Symbol](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/15/Flowchart_decision.svg/100px-Flowchart_decision.svg.png) | Decision | Represents a point where a decision is made, usually with "yes" or "no" branches. |
| ![Input/Output Symbol](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/dd/Flowchart_data.svg/100px-Flowchart_data.svg.png) | Input/Output | Represents data input (e.g., reading a value) or data output (e.g., displaying a result). |
| ![Connector Symbol](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0f/Flowchart_connector.svg/100px-Flowchart_connector.svg.png) | Connector | Used to connect different parts of a flowchart, especially when it spans multiple pages or sections. |
| ![Flow Line](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b0/Flowchart_arrow.svg/100px-Flowchart_arrow.svg.png) | Flow Line | Indicates the direction of flow or the sequence of operations. |

## Practical Scenario

Imagine you're describing a simple process for making a cup of tea:

1.  **Start** (Terminator)
2.  **Boil Water** (Process)
3.  **Put Tea Bag in Cup** (Process)
4.  **Pour Hot Water into Cup** (Input/Output)
5.  **Add Milk and Sugar?** (Decision)
    *   **Yes:**
        *   **Add Milk** (Process)
        *   **Add Sugar** (Process)
        *   Go to next step
    *   **No:**
        *   Go to next step
6.  **Stir Tea** (Process)
7.  **End** (Terminator)

Each of these steps can be represented by the corresponding flowchart symbol.

## Self-Check Questions

*   What symbol is used to indicate the beginning or end of an algorithm?
*   When you see a diamond shape in a flowchart, what type of operation is likely being represented?
*   What symbol is used to show that data is being entered or displayed?
*   What do the arrows in a flowchart represent?
