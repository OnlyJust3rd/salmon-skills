# What is an Algorithm?

An algorithm is a step-by-step procedure or a set of instructions for solving a problem or completing a task. Think of it as a recipe for a computer. Just like a recipe tells you exactly what ingredients to use and what steps to follow to bake a cake, an algorithm tells a computer what steps to follow to achieve a specific outcome.

## The Core Idea

At its heart, an algorithm needs to be:

*   **Well-defined:** Each step must be precise and unambiguous. There should be no confusion about what to do next.
*   **Finite:** The algorithm must terminate after a finite number of steps. It can't run forever.
*   **Effective:** Each step must be feasible and executable.

## A Practical Example: Making Toast

Let's consider the simple task of making toast. We can describe this as an algorithm:

1.  **Start:** Get a slice of bread.
2.  **Step 1:** Place the slice of bread into the toaster slot.
3.  **Step 2:** Select the desired toasting level (e.g., light, medium, dark).
4.  **Step 3:** Press the lever down to start toasting.
5.  **Wait:** The toaster heats the bread until it reaches the selected level.
6.  **Step 4:** The toaster pops up the toast.
7.  **End:** Remove the toast from the toaster.

This is a simple, finite, and well-defined set of instructions. If we change an instruction (e.g., "put the bread in the oven" instead of the toaster), we have a different algorithm for making toast.

## Practice Task

Think of another everyday task that can be broken down into a series of precise steps. This could be tying your shoelaces, brushing your teeth, or making a cup of tea. Write down the steps as if you were explaining it to someone who has never done it before.
