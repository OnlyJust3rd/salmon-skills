# VR and AR Characteristics

This lesson focuses on recalling the fundamental characteristics that define Virtual Reality (VR) and Augmented Reality (AR). Understanding these core concepts is essential for grasping how these advanced interaction paradigms work.

## Core Concepts of VR and AR

VR and AR are both immersive technologies, but they differ significantly in how they interact with our perception of reality.

### Virtual Reality (VR)

VR aims to create a completely simulated environment that replaces the user's real-world surroundings. When you are in VR, you are essentially transported to another digital world.

**Key Characteristics of VR:**

*   **Immersion:** The user is fully enveloped by a digital environment, blocking out the real world.
*   **Presence:** The feeling of "being there" in the virtual world, which is crucial for a convincing VR experience.
*   **Interactivity:** The ability for the user to interact with and manipulate elements within the virtual environment.
*   **Sensory Feedback:** VR often engages multiple senses, primarily sight and sound, and increasingly touch through haptic feedback.

### Augmented Reality (AR)

AR overlays digital information onto the real world. Instead of replacing reality, AR enhances it by adding virtual elements that appear to coexist with physical objects.

**Key Characteristics of AR:**

*   **Integration:** Digital information is blended with the real-world environment.
*   **Real-time Interaction:** Users can interact with both real and virtual objects in real-time.
*   **3D Registration:** Virtual objects are accurately positioned and oriented within the real world.
*   **Contextual Information:** AR can provide relevant information based on the user's location or what they are looking at.

## Practical Scenario

Imagine you're using your smartphone for two different experiences:

*   **VR Experience:** You put on a VR headset and are instantly transported to a virtual museum where you can walk around and examine exhibits. Your real room disappears, and you are fully immersed in the museum.
*   **AR Experience:** You are standing in your living room and use an AR app on your phone. You point your phone at your coffee table, and a virtual dinosaur appears to be sitting on it, interacting with the real table. You can still see your living room around the dinosaur.
