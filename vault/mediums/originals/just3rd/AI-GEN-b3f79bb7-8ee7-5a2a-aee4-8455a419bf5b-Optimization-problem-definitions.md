# Optimization Problem Definitions

In applied calculus problem-solving, optimization problems are a core concept. They involve finding the maximum or minimum value of a function under certain constraints. To tackle these effectively, we need to understand the fundamental definitions.

## Key Definitions

*   **Objective Function**: This is the function that we want to maximize or minimize. It represents the quantity we are trying to optimize (e.g., profit, area, distance, cost).
*   **Constraint(s)**: These are conditions or limitations that must be satisfied. They restrict the possible values of the variables in the objective function.
*   **Local Maximum/Minimum**: A point where the function's value is greater/less than or equal to the values at all nearby points.
*   **Absolute (Global) Maximum/Minimum**: A point where the function's value is the greatest/least over its entire domain or a specified interval.
*   **Critical Point**: A point \(c\) in the domain of a function \(f\) where either \(f'(c) = 0\) or \(f'(c)\) is undefined. Critical points are candidates for local extrema.

## Practical Scenario

Imagine a farmer wants to build a rectangular pen for their chickens. They have 100 meters of fencing material. What dimensions should the pen have to maximize the area enclosed?

Here:
*   The **objective function** is the area of the rectangle, \(A = lw\), where \(l\) is the length and \(w\) is the width.
*   The **constraint** is the total length of fencing, which is the perimeter: \(2l + 2w = 100\).

Our goal is to find the values of \(l\) and \(w\) that maximize \(A\) while satisfying the perimeter constraint.
