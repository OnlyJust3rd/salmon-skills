# Understanding the Scope of Data Processing Under PDPA

This lesson focuses on understanding the extent of data processing covered by Thailand's Personal Data Protection Act (PDPA). This is a crucial step in interpreting the PDPA's implications for how we handle personal information.

## What is "Data Processing"?

Before we can understand the scope, we need to define what "data processing" means in the context of the PDPA. The Act defines processing very broadly. It's not just about databases or sophisticated analytics.

> Processing means any operation or set of operations, whether by automated means or not, which is performed upon personal data, such as collection, recording, organisation, storage, adaptation or alteration, retrieval, consultation, use, disclosure by transmission, dissemination or otherwise making available, alignment or combination, or erasure, destruction or blocking.

Essentially, if you do *anything* with personal data, from the moment you collect it to the moment you delete it, you are processing it.

### Key Takeaways for Processing:

*   **Broad Definition:** Almost any action taken on personal data counts as processing.
*   **Automated or Manual:** It applies to both digital and physical data handling.
*   **Life Cycle Coverage:** From collection to destruction, all stages are covered.

## What Kind of Data is Covered? (Personal Data)

The PDPA applies to "personal data." This refers to any information relating to a person, which enables the identification of that person, directly or indirectly.

### Examples of Personal Data:

*   **Direct Identifiers:** Name, ID card number, passport number, telephone number, email address.
*   **Indirect Identifiers:** Location data, IP address, cookies, online identifiers, photographs, videos where a person can be identified.
*   **Sensitive Personal Data:** This is a special category and includes data like race, ethnic origin, political opinions, religious beliefs, health data, criminal records, genetic data, biometric data, sexual orientation. Processing sensitive personal data has stricter rules.

### What is NOT Personal Data?

*   **Anonymized Data:** Data that has been processed in such a way that the data subject can no longer be identified.
*   **Aggregated Data:** Data that has been combined and summarized from multiple sources, where individual identities are not discernible.

## When Does the PDPA Apply?

The PDPA's reach is extensive. It applies to the processing of personal data when:

1.  **Processing takes place in Thailand:** Regardless of where the data controller or processor is located. If the processing activity happens on Thai soil, the PDPA applies.
2.  **Offering goods or services to Data Subjects in Thailand:** Even if the processing happens outside Thailand, if you are targeting individuals *in* Thailand with your products or services, the PDPA applies to the personal data you collect and process from them.
3.  **Monitoring the behaviour of Data Subjects in Thailand:** If you track the online or offline activities of individuals located in Thailand, the PDPA applies to that monitoring and the data derived from it.

### Scenario Example:

Imagine a Thai e-commerce company ("ThaiShop") based in Bangkok.

*   **Scenario A:** ThaiShop collects customer names and addresses for order fulfillment. This is processing in Thailand. **PDPA applies.**
*   **Scenario B:** A US-based online course provider ("GlobalLearn") advertises its courses in Thai language and accepts payments in Thai Baht, targeting Thai residents. They collect names and payment details from these Thai users. Even though GlobalLearn's servers are in the US, their actions constitute offering services to data subjects in Thailand. **PDPA applies to GlobalLearn's processing of Thai users' data.**
*   **Scenario C:** A social media platform uses cookies to track user browsing habits on Thai websites to show targeted ads. This is monitoring behavior in Thailand. **PDPA applies to the platform's data processing.**

### What if an organization is not in Thailand but processes data of Thai citizens?

As seen in Scenario B and C above, the PDPA extends its reach beyond Thailand's borders if the processing activities involve offering goods/services to individuals in Thailand or monitoring their behaviour within Thailand. This is known as extraterritorial application.

## Key Considerations for Data Processing Scope:

Understanding the scope is about recognizing that the PDPA is not just for large corporations or tech companies. It applies to any entity that collects, uses, or discloses personal data in a way that falls under the Act's jurisdiction.

*   **Who is the "Data Controller" and "Data Processor"?** The PDPA distinguishes between these roles, and their responsibilities differ. The scope of processing applies to both.
*   **What is the "purpose" of processing?** The PDPA emphasizes that data must be processed for specific, legitimate purposes.
*   **Data Minimisation:** You should only collect and process the data that is necessary for the stated purpose.

By grasping the broad definition of "data processing" and the wide reach of the PDPA, you are building a strong foundation for complying with Thai data protection laws.
