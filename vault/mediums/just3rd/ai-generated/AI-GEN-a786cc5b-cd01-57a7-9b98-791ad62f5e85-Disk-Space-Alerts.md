# Disk Space Alerts

Understanding different types of infrastructure events is crucial for effective IT operations. One common and important event type that triggers alerts is related to disk space. This lesson focuses on identifying alerts generated when a system's disk storage is running low.

## What is a Disk Space Alert?

A disk space alert is a notification generated by monitoring systems when the available free space on a storage drive or partition falls below a predefined threshold. These thresholds are set to ensure that systems have sufficient room to operate and store data without interruption or performance degradation.

Think of your computer's hard drive like a physical filing cabinet. If the cabinet gets too full, you can't add new files, and it becomes difficult to find what you already have. Similarly, when a server's disk space fills up, it can lead to a variety of problems.

## Why Are Disk Space Alerts Important?

Low disk space can cause several critical issues:

*   **System Instability:** Operating systems and applications need free space to function. They use this space for temporary files, logs, swap files (virtual memory), and updates. When space is scarce, these operations can fail, leading to system crashes or unresponsiveness.
*   **Data Loss:** If a disk becomes completely full, any attempt to write new data will fail. This can result in data corruption or the inability to save important information, potentially leading to data loss.
*   **Application Failures:** Many applications, especially databases and web servers, require significant disk space for their operations. Low disk space can cause these applications to stop working or perform poorly.
*   **Performance Degradation:** Even before a system becomes completely unusable, low disk space can significantly slow down operations. The system may spend more time managing existing data and less time performing its intended tasks.
*   **Security Risks:** In some cases, full disks can prevent security updates from being installed, leaving systems vulnerable.

## Common Causes of Low Disk Space

Several factors can contribute to a disk filling up:

*   **Log File Accumulation:** System and application logs are essential for troubleshooting but can grow very large over time if not managed.
*   **Temporary Files:** Many processes generate temporary files that may not always be cleaned up automatically.
*   **Large Data Growth:** Applications that store user data, media files, or large datasets can consume space rapidly.
*   **Orphaned Files/Backups:** Old backup files or data that is no longer needed but not deleted can remain on the disk.
*   **Software Updates/Patches:** Sometimes, new software installations or updates require substantial temporary space during the process.

## Recognizing Disk Space Alerts

Disk space alerts typically appear in your monitoring dashboard or are sent via email, SMS, or other notification channels. The alert message will usually specify:

*   **The affected server or system.**
*   **The specific disk or partition that is low on space.**
*   **The amount of free space remaining (e.g., 5GB free, 95% used).**
*   **The threshold that was crossed.**

**Example Alert Message:**

```
ALERT: Low Disk Space on webserver01
Partition: /var/log
Free Space: 2.5 GB (92% used)
Threshold: 10% free space
```

## Types of Disk Space Thresholds

Monitoring systems often use different thresholds to provide escalating levels of warning:

*   **Warning Threshold:** This is the first level, indicating that disk space is becoming a concern. For example, when free space drops below 20%. This gives administrators time to investigate without immediate panic.
*   **Critical Threshold:** This is a more urgent level, signaling that immediate action is required. For example, when free space drops below 10% or 5%. At this point, system instability is a real risk.
*   **Emergency/Full Threshold:** In some systems, an alert might be triggered when the disk is 99% or 100% full. This is a critical failure state.

## What to Do When You Receive a Disk Space Alert

Upon receiving a disk space alert, the immediate steps involve investigating and taking action to free up space. This is an example of a **Remember** level skill, where the primary goal is to recall and recognize these common alerts. Further lessons will cover the actions to take.
