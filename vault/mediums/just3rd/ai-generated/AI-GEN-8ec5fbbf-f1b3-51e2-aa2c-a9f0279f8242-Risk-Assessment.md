# Identifying Competitive Threats

Understanding the purpose of competitive analysis involves recognizing why it's crucial for your business. A key part of this is identifying and understanding the threats posed by your competitors. This micro-skill focuses on developing that ability.

## What is a Competitive Threat?

A competitive threat is any action or characteristic of a competitor that could negatively impact your business's market position, profitability, or growth. These threats aren't always direct price wars; they can be subtle and evolve over time.

Think of it like a game of chess. You're not just looking at your opponent's current move, but anticipating their next few moves and how they might put your king (your business) in danger.

## Common Types of Competitive Threats:

*   **New Entrants:** Competitors entering your market, bringing fresh ideas, lower costs, or different business models.
*   **Substitute Products/Services:** Offerings from other industries that fulfill the same customer need in a different way.
*   **Bargaining Power of Buyers:** Customers who have the power to drive down prices or demand higher quality.
*   **Bargaining Power of Suppliers:** Suppliers who can raise prices or reduce the quality of inputs, impacting your costs.
*   **Rivalry Among Existing Competitors:** Intense competition within your current market, leading to price wars, aggressive marketing, or product innovation races.

## Practical Scenario: A Local Coffee Shop

Imagine you own a small, independent coffee shop in a busy neighborhood.

*   **Initial Situation:** You have loyal customers, a cozy atmosphere, and good quality coffee.
*   **Threat 1 (New Entrant):** A large, well-funded coffee chain announces they are opening a branch just two blocks away. They have a strong brand name, offer loyalty programs, and can likely afford to run initial promotions below cost.
    *   *Your Understanding:* This chain can attract customers with their brand recognition and potentially steal market share through aggressive pricing or convenience.
*   **Threat 2 (Substitute):** A new bakery opens across the street that also serves excellent coffee, along with pastries. People who used to grab coffee from you before work might now stop at the bakery for both their caffeine and a treat.
    *   *Your Understanding:* This bakery is a substitute offering that captures a customer need (coffee and breakfast/snack) that you previously fulfilled exclusively.
*   **Threat 3 (Rivalry):** One of your established independent competitors, known for its budget-friendly prices, starts offering a "buy one, get one free" deal every Tuesday.
    *   *Your Understanding:* This direct competitor is intensifying rivalry through a promotional strategy that directly targets your price-sensitive customers.

By identifying these threats, you can start thinking about how to respond. For instance, you might:

*   Emphasize your unique selling points (artisanal quality, community focus) against the chain.
*   Develop new signature drinks or pastries to compete with the bakery.
*   Introduce your own loyalty program or a "happy hour" special to counter the competitor's promotion.

## Practice Task: Identify Threats for a Fictional Business

Let's say you're analyzing a fictional online subscription box service that delivers gourmet snacks.

Consider these potential scenarios and identify the competitive threat each represents:

1.  A company launches a similar snack box service but focuses exclusively on vegan and organic options, which your service doesn't.
2.  Your primary shipping provider significantly increases their delivery fees.
3.  A popular influencer starts promoting a new type of DIY snack-making kit that customers can assemble themselves at home.
4.  Two of your main competitors merge, creating a larger, more dominant entity in the market.
