# Conversational Technology Design: Applying Workspace Awareness and Social Presence

In conversational technologies, **workspace awareness** refers to understanding what others are doing or have done within a shared digital space. **Social presence** is the feeling of being "there" with other people, even when physically apart. For conversational agents, these concepts help make interactions feel more natural and collaborative.

## Understanding the Application

When designing conversational technologies, we need to consider how users perceive the agent and other potential users. This involves:

*   **Indicating Agent Activity:** Letting users know when the agent is typing, processing information, or has completed a task. This mimics human conversational cues.
*   **Showing Shared Context:** If multiple users are interacting with the agent, or if the agent is accessing shared data, making this visible helps everyone understand the current state.
*   **Mimicking Social Cues:** Using natural language, emojis, or even subtle pauses can convey a sense of personality and social connection.

## Practical Example: A Collaborative Meeting Assistant

Imagine a conversational AI assisting a team during a virtual meeting.

**Scenario:** A team is using a chatbot to manage their project tasks.

1.  **Workspace Awareness:**
    *   When one team member asks the assistant to "add a task: Research competitor pricing," the assistant might respond with: "Okay, I'm adding 'Research competitor pricing' to the backlog. *[Assistant typing indicator appears]*"
    *   Later, another team member says, "Assign 'Research competitor pricing' to Sarah." The assistant replies, "Got it, Sarah is now assigned to 'Research competitor pricing'." If Sarah is also in the chat, she might see a notification directly.

2.  **Social Presence:**
    *   If the meeting runs long, the assistant could say, "Looks like we're running a bit over time, team! Should I remind everyone about the next agenda item, or continue discussing the current task?" This phrasing creates a sense of shared experience.
    *   When a task is completed, the assistant could say, "Great job, team! The 'Finalize Q3 report' task is now marked as complete."
