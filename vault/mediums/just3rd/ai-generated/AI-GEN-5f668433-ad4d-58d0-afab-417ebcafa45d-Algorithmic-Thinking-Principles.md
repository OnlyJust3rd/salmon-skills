# Algorithmic Thinking Principles

Algorithmic thinking is about breaking down complex problems into a series of simple, manageable steps. Think of it as creating a recipe for solving a problem. Each step must be clear, unambiguous, and lead you closer to the final solution.

### Key Concepts

*   **Sequence:** Steps are performed in a specific order, one after another.
*   **Selection (or Conditionals):** Making decisions based on certain conditions. "If this is true, do that; otherwise, do something else."
*   **Iteration (or Loops):** Repeating a set of steps multiple times, either a fixed number of times or until a certain condition is met.
*   **Decomposition:** Breaking down a large problem into smaller, more manageable sub-problems.
*   **Pattern Recognition:** Identifying similarities or recurring elements within a problem that can be solved with the same approach.

### Practical Example: Making a Cup of Tea

Let's apply these principles to a common task: making a cup of tea.

1.  **Sequence:**
    *   Boil water.
    *   Place a teabag in a mug.
    *   Pour boiling water into the mug.
    *   Let it steep.
    *   Remove the teabag.
    *   Add milk and/or sugar (optional).

2.  **Selection:**
    *   **IF** you want milk, **THEN** add milk.
    *   **IF** you want sugar, **THEN** add sugar.

3.  **Iteration:**
    *   Imagine you need to make tea for 10 people. You would *repeat* the process of making a cup of tea 10 times.

4.  **Decomposition:**
    *   The overall problem is "Make a cup of tea."
    *   Sub-problems include: "Boil water," "Steep tea," "Add additions."
